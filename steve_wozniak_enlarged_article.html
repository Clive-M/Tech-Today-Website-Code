Apple Co-founder Steve Wozniak Hospitalized in Mexico City

Reports are emerging that Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak, also known as Woz, has been hospitalized in Mexico City. The 73-year-old tech pioneer was attending the World Business Forum (WBF) when the incident occurred, details of which remain unclear. According to an unnamed source from the WBF, Wozniak fainted on Wednesday afternoon during the business conference. The cause of his sudden illness is currently unknown, and there has been no official statement from Wozniak's representatives. The BBC reached out for comment, but as of now, there has been no response. Wozniak, a key figure in Silicon Valley, played a pivotal role in the founding of Apple alongside Steve Jobs in 1976. Together, they introduced the world to the first Apple computer, and the company went on to become the most valuable in the world. This incident comes at a time when Wozniak has been actively advocating for ethical considerations in technology. In March, he joined forces with Elon Musk to call for a pause in the development of highly advanced artificial intelligence (AI) models. Wozniak expressed concerns about the potential misuse of AI technology by malicious entities, emphasizing the need for regulatory measures. Speaking to the BBC in May 2023, Wozniak highlighted the intelligence of AI, making it susceptible to exploitation by "bad actors" who aim to deceive individuals. He urged for regulatory frameworks to be put in place to address the risks associated with AI development. However, he also expressed skepticism about the effectiveness of regulatory efforts, stating, "I think the forces that drive for money usually win out, which is sort of sad." As the tech community and Apple enthusiasts await updates on Wozniak's health, the incident serves as a reminder of the ongoing challenges and ethical considerations surrounding advancements in technology.
